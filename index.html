About

I am a PhD student at Lancaster University, United Kingdom, working with Prof. Andrew Binley. My PhD project is affiliated with Dr. Oliver Kuras from the geophysical tomography team at the British Geological Survey. I spent two months of my PhD visiting Dr. Tim Johnson at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (USA). Prior to my doctoral research, I worked with Prof. Jim Yeh on hydraulic tomography and obtained an MS from the University of Arizona in the USA.

Since April 2019, I work as a RA data scientist at the UK Centre for Ecology and Hydrology in Lancaster.

My research interests include:

  • Environmental data science
  • Sensor networks
  • Aquifer characterization
  • Geophysical monitoring of subsurface fluid migration
  • Data assimilation (multiple data types, snapshots etc.)
  • Time series analysis
  • Geostatistics
  • Petrophysics
  • Recharge and vadose zone processes
  • Groundwater modelling
  • Uncertainty quantification

For more information see my complete curriculum vitae and a list of scientific publications.
